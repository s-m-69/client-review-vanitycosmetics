Context
The Situation
The Reality

"Dr. Santosh Bhatia has the credentials, the career, and the clinical results to be the most recognisable cosmetic surgeon brand in Mumbai. The digital footprint does not reflect this — and it is costing leads, press coverage, and trust every single day."

What exists
  • Two separate websites with no shared strategy
  • A personal brand site with a broken build (German nav, church template)
  • A clinic site with strong SEO infrastructure but undermined by discount offers
  • Fragmented social handles across both properties
  • Zero press-readiness on either site
What's being wasted
  • MCh qualification + global career narrative — invisible on homepage
  • Celebrity clientele association (YRF Studios) — buried in body copy
  • 2,000+ surgeries and 10+ years experience — not surfaced as proof
  • Kokilaben + Breach Candy hospital affiliations — not in the hero
  • 3D Crisalix technology — undersold as a differentiator
Diagnosis
The Core Problem
The fundamental disconnect

The two sites currently operate as independent islands — they share a doctor, a clinic, a phone number, and a WhatsApp link, but they have no shared brand language, no deliberate patient journey between them, and no strategy for how a visitor on one site should be directed to, or primed for, the other.

Site 1 problem: the personal brand site
  • Functions as a premium brand aspiration with none of the execution. Broken template, dead navigation links, German-language mobile menu, no real procedure content.
  • Has a lightbox-calendar booking flow (a genuine asset) but no SEO, no procedure pages, no PR infrastructure.
  • Should be the doctor's editorial platform — the place where Dr. Bhatia's story, philosophy, and authority are told. Currently it is none of these things.
Site 2 problem: the clinic site
  • Has the right SEO architecture but sabotages its own positioning by placing discount offers next to medical credentials — a fundamental brand contradiction.
  • Is the operational site — procedure pages, before/after gallery, blog — but the booking flow is less sophisticated than the personal site. This is backwards.
  • Should be the clinical conversion engine — the place patients go to research procedures, read results, and book. It is close to this but not there yet.
Strategy
Strategic Vision
The Guiding Principle

"The personal site builds desire and trust. The clinic site converts it. Every visitor who lands on either site should leave knowing exactly who Dr. Santosh Bhatia is, why he is the right choice, and how to book. The two sites are chapters in the same story — not separate publications."

Site 1 — New Role: The Authority Platform
  • Dr. Bhatia's editorial home — his story, philosophy, and voice
  • The brand's PR surface — press-ready, media kit, shareable
  • The trust-builder — credentials, celebrity angle, global career, before/after
  • Funnels warm visitors to the clinic site to complete research and book
Site 2 — New Role: The Conversion Engine
  • The operational clinic hub — all procedures, all services
  • The SEO workhorse — ranking for every high-intent procedure keyword in Mumbai
  • The conversion machine — lightbox booking, lead capture, WhatsApp flow
  • Cross-links back to personal site for trust-building content
Shared Brand Architecture
  • One visual identity system — same typography, colour palette, tone of voice, and photography direction across both sites.
  • One brand hierarchy — "Dr. Santosh Bhatia at Vanity Cosmetic Clinic" — with clear visual subordination between personal brand and clinic brand.
  • Unified social handle strategy — aligned across both sites, all platforms, with consistent cross-linking.
  • Shared proof assets — 2,000+ surgeries, global career, hospital affiliations, and celebrity clientele surfaced consistently across both sites, not siloed to one.

"A patient Googles 'rhinoplasty Mumbai.' They land on the clinic site procedure page (SEO). They research. They click through to Dr. Bhatia's personal site to verify his credentials (trust). They return to the clinic site to book (conversion). Both sites play a distinct, deliberate role in the same journey."
